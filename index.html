vision

where i want to take us

To rebuild CURSOR as a community where every CS major feels seen, supported, and proud to belong — not just as a member, but as a contributor to something that matters.

CURSOR has the talent, the history, and the heart — but we've been drifting. Member renewals are down. Participation has slipped. And too many of our fellow CS majors feel like the org isn't for them anymore. That changes this term.

My vision is simple: put people first. Every event, every program, every decision will be made with our members at the center. Because a strong CURSOR isn't built on numbers — it's built on genuine connection, shared growth, and a culture where everyone has a reason to show up.

platform

goals, strategies & plans of action

Three words. One direction. PULL the community back together. PUSH every member to their full potential. COMMIT to a CURSOR that lasts.

We've lost members and connections. PULL is about bringing people back — renewing bonds, rebuilding numbers, and making every CS major feel like CURSOR is theirs again.
P

Pursue Growth in Numbers

Goal: Reverse membership decline through active, intentional recruitment
Strategy
  • Target incoming CS majors and lapsed members with personal, peer-to-peer outreach
Plans of Action
  • Set up better orientation booths and CS department partnerships
  • Launch a referral program rewarding members who recruit peers
  • Offer a free first-event experience to lower the barrier to joining
U

Unite Disconnected Members

Goal: Rebuild bonds between members who've grown distant or disengaged
Strategy
  • Create shared cross-batch experiences that bridge year levels and backgrounds
Plans of Action
  • Host cross-batch mixers and year-level collaboration events
  • Launch a Big-Little(endian) mentorship pairing for new and returning members
  • Build moderated group spaces for ongoing peer connection
L

Listen to the Membership

Goal: Understand what members actually need — not what we assume they want
Strategy
  • Build formal and informal feedback channels into every org touchpoint
Plans of Action
  • Run semestral anonymous member surveys
  • Hold open-forum town halls at the start and end of each term
  • Create a permanent anonymous suggestion channel for ongoing input
L

Lure Back Lapsed Members & Alumni

Goal: Re-engage members who stopped showing up — and rebuild alumni ties that have quietly faded
Strategy
  • Reach out personally to both inactive members and alumni — make re-entry easy, welcoming, and genuinely worth it
Plans of Action
  • Run a "Come Back to CURSOR" campaign each semester start for lapsed members
  • Offer returning members a reduced renewal fee and a welcome-back event
  • Build an alumni directory and reconnect through personal outreach
  • Invite alumni to career talks, mentorship sessions, and org milestones
  • Host an annual alumni homecoming to keep the bond alive
CURSOR is full of untapped talent and underutilized skills. PUSH is about unlocking that potential — giving every member a stage, a challenge, and a reason to grow.
P

Platform for Hidden Talent

Goal: Give every member a visible space to showcase what they can do
Strategy
  • Create low-barrier, high-visibility opportunities for members to share their work and skills
Plans of Action
  • Host semestral internal demo days and project showcases
  • Launch a member spotlight series on org social media
  • Feature member projects in a public CURSOR portfolio page
U

Upskill Every Member

Goal: Help members grow their CS skills beyond the classroom
Strategy
  • Run peer-led and industry-connected learning programs in high-demand tech areas
Plans of Action
  • Launch a workshop series covering web dev, AI/ML, and competitive programming
  • Organize internal hackathons and coding challenges
  • Form peer study groups by interest and skill level
S

Support Career Readiness

Goal: Prepare members for internships, jobs, and real-world CS careers
Strategy
  • Connect members with alumni, industry partners, and career development resources
Plans of Action
  • Host resume reviews and mock technical interviews
  • Build an alumni mentorship pipeline for career advice
  • Partner with companies for tech talks, job referrals, and internship leads
H

Highlight Member Achievements

Goal: Build a culture where wins — big and small — are celebrated publicly
Strategy
  • Make recognition a consistent, visible part of CURSOR's identity and culture
Plans of Action
  • Feature member wins, certifications, and projects on org platforms
  • Host a semestral awards night recognizing member contributions
  • Build a running leaderboard of org contributions and participation
PULL and PUSH mean nothing without follow-through. COMMIT is the promise — consistent, transparent, inclusive leadership that holds itself accountable to every member, every term.
C

Consistent Programming

Goal: Deliver reliable, high-quality events and programs every semester
Strategy
  • Plan and lock in a full events calendar before each term begins
Plans of Action
  • Publish a semestral events calendar in the first week of term
  • Establish a dedicated events committee with clear ownership
  • Run post-event evaluations to continuously improve quality
O

Open & Transparent Leadership

Goal: Ensure members always know what the org is doing and why
Strategy
  • Proactively communicate org decisions, updates, and finances to the general membership
Plans of Action
  • Publish monthly officer updates and meeting minutes
  • Implement an open-door policy across all officer positions
  • Share budget summaries and event outcomes with members
M

Member-Driven Decisions

Goal: Make sure member voices actively shape what CURSOR does
Strategy
  • Involve members in planning and priority-setting — not just as attendees but as decision-makers
Plans of Action
  • Hold participatory planning sessions at the start of each term
  • Put major org decisions to a member vote
  • Form a member advisory council as a permanent feedback body
M

Meaningful Internal Culture

Goal: Make CURSOR feel like a second home, not just an organization
Strategy
  • Invest in the relationships between members — not just the activities on the calendar
Plans of Action
  • Launch a member care program with regular check-ins
  • Recognize birthdays, milestones, and personal wins publicly
  • Build officer-to-member relationships beyond event logistics
I

Inclusive for Everyone

Goal: Ensure every CS major feels welcome — regardless of year, background, or skill level
Strategy
  • Actively design programs and spaces that reach underrepresented and often-overlooked members
Plans of Action
  • Train officers in inclusive leadership and safe space practices
  • Run a buddy system pairing new members with a welcoming point of contact
  • Ensure events are accessible, affordable, and varied in format
T

Track & Measure Our Progress

Goal: Hold ourselves publicly accountable to every commitment we make
Strategy
  • Set clear, measurable metrics at the start of term and report on them honestly at the end
Plans of Action
  • Publish a semester-end report card on renewal rates, attendance, and satisfaction
  • Set public goals each term and review them openly with members
  • Use member satisfaction surveys to grade every major initiative
B

Build Bridges Across the Org

Goal: Create a CURSOR where every member feels genuinely welcome — not just tolerated, but included
⚠ honest acknowledgment We know this won't be easy. Low member participation makes it hard to get people in the same room, and a limited budget means we have to be creative with what we can offer. We're naming these upfront — and designing around them.
Strategy
  • Design events with built-in mixing — small groups, shared goals, and cross-batch collaboration that make new connections feel natural, not forced
Plans of Action
  • Run small-group events (6–12 people) where everyone has to engage
  • Assign cross-batch teams for org activities and collaborative projects
  • Prioritize free or low-cost formats — game nights, study jams, potlucks — to work within budget
  • Train officers to actively welcome and introduce unfamiliar members at every event
